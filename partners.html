Partners

The National Youth Program for Skills Development is keen on identifying the highest standards in selecting the Program’s partners; in order to provide the best experience that lives up to the aspirations of the Omani youth based on the 4th Industrial Revolution and its skills.

Therefore, we partnered with the educational and developmental institutions topping the international specialized classifications; in order to provide the latest scientific and technical approaches and practices in the world. Furthermore, engagements have been made with a number of leading Omani institutions that have enriched the Program with local experiences and achieved balance for the participants between the international and national experiences.

Google is the world's leading company in online search engines, and the global organizer for the enormous amount of information on the web. The Company offers many services for the smart technology developers and also provides advanced digital solutions for the owners of startup technology companies; here the National Youth Program for Skills Development’s team worked with a dedicated team from Google Inc. to design and develop the "Omani Youth Tech Ideathon" and its educational content.

https://developers.google.com/

Udacity is one of the leading institutions in providing and developing the (4IR) virtual education. In its search for the best, the National Youth Program for Skills Development’s team worked with Udacity to organize the “Al-Shabab scheme – Stage One”; as this unique scheme was designed by experts working in the most efficient and influential companies in the world, such as Google, Amazon, IBM, and Mercedes. Participants are taught one of the three constituent materials, all of which lead to obtaining the Nanodegree.

https://www.udacity.com/

The Federal Institute of Technology in Lausanne, Switzerland, is one of the best educational institutions and enjoys high prestige for teaching (4IR) skills and knowledge. It is also very convenient for working with the National Youth Program for Skills Development’s team to organize the Al Shabab Scheme – Stage Two; thus giving the participants a chance to learn about the latest ideas and innovations in (4IR) and startup technology companies field.

https://www.epfl.ch/

The Nanyang Technological University in Singapore is considered one of the most prominent universities that focus on teaching skills and providing the knowledge required by the Fourth Industrial Revolution (4IR). It works with the National Youth Program for Skills Development’s team to organize the Al Shabab Scheme – Stage Two. The (NTU) colleges, teaching staff and students are the most prominent workers and academics in the (4IR) field.

http://www.ntu.edu.sg/

The National University of Singapore ranked 11th in the QS World University Rankings in 2019, and the World University Ranking issued by the Times Higher Education (THE) has placed it in the 22nd world rank in 2018. It is considered the top Asian university in both rankings. The Program’s team has worked on designing an exceptional experience for the Al Shabab Scheme’s participants through interactive workshops and business accelerators.

http://www.nus.edu.sg/

http://outwardboundoman.com/
